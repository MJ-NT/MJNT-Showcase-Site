Katabasis

You walk in night, through vaulted chambers and secluded caverns, through passageways, hewn from knowing stone. There is water, at times, clear and cold. There is quiet, too, and dark.

What choice drew you to chthonic depths?

What private hymn still guides your steps, thunder and hum? Expresses blood to limbs. Carries life to muscles sore from miles travelled. What else could drive a heart but love?

The old clichés hold weight: in truisms, truth.

And so you walk. Time has been afforded to reason, question, and doubt, each and all cast aside in the inexorable rhythm of your footfalls.

What choice?

It was no choice at all.

But what is the nature of your love? What lends acid to its absence?

Daylight

Your love is the sun. It branded you from conception to fiery end. Its searing radiance burns you still.

For when the object of your affection was gone, your love burned all the brighter. It drove you downwards to seek its heat, acted shepherd and guide to you.

Poor perennial.
Desperate heliotrope.

That is your love.

Dreamt

Your love does not blaze. It is not meteor, but inner kindling.

An inward confession.

Quiet certainty in the shared moments that are foundation and edifice.

Affirming ember of remembrance, you walk.

That is your love.

Delicate

Your love was a fragile thing, but no less beautiful for this fragility. You were cradle and nest, heart and harbour. You nurtured this fledgling creature, fed and raised it to gently-prompted flight.

And when your love took wing, it soared. So in falling, your love dove deep. Caught, coped, clipped, and caged.

But you will bend the bars, and you will strike the lock, and you will nurture once again.

That is your love.
